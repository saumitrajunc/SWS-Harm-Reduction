How to Use Narcan

Narcan (naloxone) is easy to use and can save a life. Here's what you need to know:

Signs of Overdose

  • Unresponsive or unconscious
  • Slow, shallow, or no breathing
  • Blue or gray lips/fingernails
  • Pinpoint pupils
  • Cold, clammy skin
  • Snoring or gurgling sounds

Steps to Administer Narcan

  • Call 911 immediately
  • Check for breathing and pulse
  • Administer Narcan (intranasal or intramuscular)
  • Perform rescue breathing if needed
  • Stay with the person until help arrives
  • Administer second dose if no response in 3-5 minutes

Good Samaritan Laws

North Carolina's Good Samaritan law protects individuals who seek medical help for drug overdoses, including those who administer Narcan.

Emergency Protocol

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Narcan access and use.

Common Questions

  • Is Narcan really free? Yes, completely free with no questions asked.
  • Do I need training? Basic instructions are provided, but formal training is recommended.
  • Can I get in trouble for having Narcan? No, it's legal to possess and use Narcan.
  • What if I use it and it doesn't work? Call 911 and administer a second dose if available.
  • How long does Narcan last? Effects last 30-90 minutes, so medical care is still needed.
  • Can I get more kits? Yes, you can get additional kits anytime.
